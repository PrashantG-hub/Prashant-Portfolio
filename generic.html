    Spotify

    This project analyzes Spotify’s music store data using SQL to understand customer behavior, music preferences, and revenue trends. The goal was to extract meaningful business insights by querying multiple related tables such as customers, invoices, tracks, artists, and genres.

  • View Project
    • Started with simple queries to understand the dataset, such as identifying the senior-most employee, countries with the most invoices, top invoice values, and the best-performing cities and customers based on total spending.
    Analyzed customer listening behavior by identifying rock music listeners, ordering them by email, and determining which artists produced the most rock tracks. This involved multiple joins across customer, invoice, track, album, and genre tables. Compared individual track durations against the average song length to identify longer tracks, helping understand content distribution in the catalog. Used Common Table Expressions (CTEs) to calculate how much each customer spent on specific artists and to identify top-selling artists. Applied window functions (ROW_NUMBER) to determine the most popular music genre per country and identify top-spending customers per country, including handling ties correctly.
    The project delivered clear insights into customer spending patterns, popular genres by country, top artists, and high-value customers, showcasing strong SQL skills in joins, subqueries, CTEs, and window functions.

    WalMart

    This project focuses on analyzing Walmart sales data using SQL to understand customer behavior, product performance, and sales trends. The objective was to perform end-to-end analysis—from data loading and feature engineering to exploratory data analysis and business insights.

  • View Project
    • Derived new analytical columns such as:

    Day Name
    Month Name
